Ways to Reach Petra

Petra, the ancient city in Jordan, can be reached by various means:

  • By Air:
    • Fly to Amman: Queen Alia International Airport (AMM) in Amman, Jordan, is the primary international airport.
    • From Amman to Petra:
      • Domestic Flights: While there are no direct flights to Petra, you can take a domestic flight from Amman to Aqaba, a city closer to Petra. From Aqaba, you can travel by road to Petra.
  • By Road:
    • From Amman to Petra:
      • Private Car or Taxi: The most common way to get to Petra from Amman is by road. You can hire a private car or taxi for the journey, which takes around 3 hours.
      • Bus: Tourist buses and public minibusses also operate between Amman and Petra, though they might take longer due to multiple stops.
    • From Aqaba to Petra:
      • Taxi or Car: If you fly into Aqaba, you can hire a taxi or car to reach Petra. The journey takes around 1.5 to 2 hours.
  • Tips:
    • Transportation: Road travel is the most common way to reach Petra. Private cars or taxis offer flexibility, but buses are also an affordable option.
    • Traveling Time: Consider the time it takes to travel from your arrival point (Amman or Aqaba) to Petra. Plan accordingly.
    • Entrance Tickets: You can buy tickets at the Visitor Center in Petra. Hiring a guide at Petra can greatly enhance your experience.
    • Check Travel Advisories: Ensure you check the latest travel advisories, especially concerning entry requirements, before traveling to Jordan.
